Competition Structure

Round 1: Qualifications

  • Format: MCQs, theoretical questions, and basic coding exercises
  • Focus: Foundational computational techniques and subject knowledge
  • Duration: 1.5 hours
  • Scoring: Automated grading
  • Advancement: Top X% qualify for Round 2 & training

Training Round

  • Format: Self-paced interactive challenges
  • Focus: Programming, computational thinking, and scientific modeling
  • Duration: Flexible (2–4 weeks)
  • Purpose: Preparation for advanced rounds

Round 2: National

  • Format: Written solutions, coding implementations, and simulations
  • Focus: Real-world problem applications
  • Duration: 2.5–3 hours
  • Scoring: Automated & expert grading
  • Advancement: Top teams proceed to finals

Round 3: Finals

  • Format: Open-ended computational science challenge (5–6 hours)
  • Focus: Advanced problem-solving using simulations & data science
  • Judging Criteria:
    • Innovation (25%)
    • Scientific Accuracy (25%)
    • Algorithm Efficiency (25%)
    • Presentation (25%)

About EOCS

The Egyptian Olympiad in Computational Science (EOCS) bridges Egypt's rich scientific heritage with cutting-edge computational innovation. We challenge students to solve complex scientific problems using modern computational methods.

Physics Computation

Quantum mechanics, particle physics, and astronomical simulations

Chemical Modeling

Molecular dynamics, reaction kinetics, and drug design

Biological Systems

DNA analysis, protein folding, and ecosystem modeling

Eligibility & Rules

High School

  • Students in grades 10-12
  • Age: 15-18 years
  • Team size: 2-3 members
  • School approval required

University

  • Undergraduate students
  • Age: 18-22 years
  • Team size: 2-4 members
  • Valid university ID required

Competition Structure

The competition consists of three main rounds plus a training round, each designed to test different aspects of computational science knowledge and problem-solving abilities.

Round 1: Qualifications

Online, Individual

1.5 Hours

  • Multiple-choice questions
  • Basic coding exercises
  • Automated grading
  • Top performers qualify

Training Round

Online, Team-Based

2-4 Weeks

  • Learning resources
  • Practice problems
  • Mentor support
  • Team building

Round 2: National Competition

Online, Team-Based

2.5-3 Hours

  • Complex problem-solving
  • Numerical simulations
  • Expert evaluation
  • Finals qualification

Round 3: Finals

Onsite, Team-Based

5-6 Hours

  • Innovation (25%)
  • Scientific Accuracy (25%)
  • Algorithm Efficiency (25%)
  • Presentation (25%)

Judging Criteria

Innovation

Creative approaches to problem-solving and unique solution methods.

25%

Scientific Accuracy

Correct application of scientific principles and computational methods.

25%

Algorithm Efficiency

Optimal use of computational resources and elegant code solutions.

25%

Presentation

Clear explanation of solution approach and implementation details.

25%
